The Messenger of Allah, Muhammad (SAW)

A glance into the life of Muhammad (SAW)

Early Life

  • 570 A.D - Prophet Muhammad was born in the year of the elephant in Makkah. His father Abdullah ibn Abd al-Muttalib died about two months before he was given birth to. As being born into a prestigious tribe, Quraysh, he was handed over to a wet nurse, where he remained for 5 years.
  • 576 A.D - After 5 years, he returned to his mother, Aminah. She took him to visit her uncles in Yathrib (now known as Madinah). She set out for Makkah after staying for a month but she died on the way. The prophet was then brought up first by his grandfather, Abd al-Muttalib, and after by his uncle, Abu Talib.

Youth and Marriage

  • 595 A.D - Khadijah bint Khuwaylid, a widow and a merchant, employed the Prophet (SAW) to sell her goods. Due to the honesty and diligence displayed by the Prophet in the selling of her goods, she proposed marriage to him, which he accepted, and they gotten married.

Beginning of Prophethood

  • 610 A.D - Jibril revealed the first few verses of the Quran to the prophet while he was thinking about purpose of his existence and God in the Cave of Hira near Makkah.
  • 613 A.D - The prophet started with the calling of people to Islam in secret. Khadijah, Ali, Abu Bakr and Zayd were one of the first to accept his calling and become Muslims. After a while he started calling the people of Makkah to Islam publicly, most didn’t respond to the call. Most of Makkans opposed him and started treating him and his followers with scorn and cruelty.
  • 615 A.D - To escape the people's cruelty, some of the followers of the Prophet left their homes and went to Ethiopia where a Christian king helped them.

Maltreatment and Boycott

  • 616 A.D - The persecution increased and Makkans decided to boycott the prophet and his clan(Banu Hashim). The prophet and his clan had to live in a valley with no source of food other than leaves and roots of trees.
  • 619 A.D - After the boycott was lifted, the prophet’s uncle Abu Talib and Khadijah died. Their health had deteriorated severely during the boycott period.
  • 620 A.D - The prophet went to Ta’if to seek help, but the people rejected him and the street urchins pelt him with stones till he was wounded over and blood soaked in his shoes.

The Night Journey and The Ascent

  • 621 A.D - The Isra and Miraj took place. The prophet was taken on a horse-like creature with wings, brought to him by Jibril, from Makkah to Jerusalem. From the Temple mount in Jerusalem, he was accompanied by Jibril to the Seven Heavens where he met other prophets and came into Allah’s presence.

Hijrah to Madinah

  • 622 A.D - The Makkans came to the decision that to halt the growth of Islam, they had to kill the prophet. So they hatched a plan to kill the Prophet. To save his life, the Prophet left for Madinah with most of his followers where they received a warm welcome from the people of Madinah. This migration marked the beginning of the hijrah calender.

Battles

  • 623 A.D - A large army of the Makkans came to Madinah to attack the Muslims but they were defeated at Badr near Madinah by the outnumbered Muslims.
  • 625 A.D - The Muslims were defeated at Uhud outside Madinah. Even the Prophet was wounded.

Peace Treaty

  • 628 A.D - The prophet signed the peace treaty at Hudaybiyah near Makkah, which the Quran called a Clear Victory.
  • 630 A.D - The Quraysh violated the treaty which led to the prophet announcing the treaty being revoked. The Prophet and 10, 000 companions marched to Makkah in complete secrecy and the Makkans being completely unaware and unprepared were helpless against the numerical strength and had to surrender in defeat. The prophet forgave all the Makkan opponents. The idols were removed from the Kabah and the people of Makkah all accepted Islam.

Farewell Piligrimage

  • 632 A.D - The prophet went for his Hajj also known as farewell pilgrimage. As he stood at the Arafat to deliver his lecture, the last verse of the Quran was revealed to him.
    "Today I have completed your religion for you, and completed My blessings upon you. I have chosen for you Islam as your religion." - Surah Al Ma'idah verse 3
  • 633 A.D - The prophet died.
